How is proffesional sport harmful for athletes?

Fit but unhealthy?

Physical health risks

The intense physical nature of sports exposes athletes to a heightened risk of injuries, ranging from minor sprains to severe, career-threatening injuries.High-impact sports like football and rugby pose particular risks due to frequent collisions and trauma.

Mental health struggles

Athletes grapple with performance anxiety and stress, driven by the pressure to excel and maintain lucrative contracts and endorsements.Fear of failure and the constant scrutiny from fans, media, and coaches contribute to a toxic environment that undermines mental well-being.

Athletes may turn to drugs and alcohol as coping mechanisms to manage the physical and emotional stresses of their careers, leading to dependency and addiction.Painkillers are commonly used to manage injuries but can result in substance abuse and long-term health consequences.
